Krishnamohan Pasumarthy: Padmasri Hemchandra Goswamy (01-03-1958)

Hailing from Assam Hem Chandra Goswami is a stalwart sattriya artist and is well known for his innovation and skill. So fascinating were the masks and their making that he started learning the craft when he was merely 10. His father, Rudrakanta Deva Goswami, a prominent mask maker and sattriya artist was his guru. Watching him closely he learnt the art at every stage.

He took the bachelor degree in Arts and Crafts from Arts and Crafts Society of Guwahati. He was acquainted with the environment of satra, the Vaishnavite monastery of Assam. He not only mastered the age-old skill but has also played a pioneering role by incorporating innovations in the mask including the speaking and movable masks and some with moving eyes. The masks depicting Narasimha, Hanuman and Garuda with their finely etched features while keeping the audience in awe, convey their personality emphatically. Such is the magic wielded by him.

He delivered lectures on the heritage of split bamboo mask in different institutions like Viswa Bharathi, Shantiniketan etc. He established an institute on Sattriya Art and culture in Chamaguri Satra Majuli to teach the new generation about the heritage of mask making. He was honoured with Sahitya Natak award and Padmasri.

Explaining the mask-making process Hemchandra says: “We start by using bamboo sticks to make the framework of the face. Then the sticky potters’ clay smeared on cotton cloth strips are pasted on the frame. A mix of clay and cow dung is used to etch out the contours of the specific character. Thereafter, a cloth is wrapped to complete the shape. The final step is colouring and polishing. Either natural colours from flowers, leaves and tree bark are used or those available in the market.”a

Inspired by his act the rest of the platoon captured the Area Flat Top.  Sanjay Kumar displayed most conspicuous gallantry, cool courage and devotion to duty of an exceptionally high order and was awarded Param Veer Chakra, the highest gallantry award.

Krishnamohan Pasumarthy: INS Vikrant (1961) INS stands for Indian Naval Ship.

INS Vikrant was an aircraft carrier of the Indian Navy. The ship was laid down as HMS Hercules for the British Royal Navy during World War II, but was put on hold when the war ended. India purchased the incomplete carrier in 1957 and construction was completed in 1961. Vikrant was commissioned as the first aircraft carrier of the Indian Navy on 4 March 1961 in Belfast by Vijaya Lakshmi Pandit, the Indian High Commissioner to the United Kingdom and played a key role in enforcing the naval blockade of East Pakistan during the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971 when as it was assigned to the Eastern Naval Command, then to the Eastern Fleet. It was decommissioned in January 1997 and was preserved as a museum ship in Naval Docks, Mumbai until 2012. It was sold through online auction and scrapped in November 2014 after final clearance from the Supreme.

HAMSA Anjani Kumar: Ashes & Riot of Colours

The celebration of paradox colours and ashes marks the essence of the festival of Holi. It embodies the essence of Sanatana Dharma and the Indian society. It teaches by way of the celebration of vibrant colours and decimation of colours, the unity and Diversity; the past and future and the life and death etc. Holi Celebrations underline physical, Temporal and philosophical nuances.

At physical level, we celebrate the resplendence of colours. By performing the ritual of Holika Dahan we accept that all colours are same in the end when they are burnt; they merge back with the the soil; all the colours would rise from the same soil. Holika Dahan serves as a powerful metaphor for transformation.

At the social level, as we celebrate Holi, we honor the principles of unity, diversity, and accommodation that underpin Hinduism and Indian culture. We recognize that our unique identities are essential to the richness of the whole. just as the various colors of Holi come together to create a kaleidoscope, different religions, cultures, languages etc contribute to the completeness of the country.

At the personal level, Holi teaches us that life is a mixture of colors, each representing the diverse experiences and emotions that shape our existence. Just as all colors combine to form white, our individual experiences merge to create a unified whole. Burning dead would reminds us to let go of our past and spraying colours asks us to embrace the future with renewed vigor.

Spiritually, it encourages us to burn away our desires and ego, paving the way for spiritual growth. Holi makes us embrace the enigma of Life i.e. the beauty of transformation; the unity of diversity, and the eternal cycle of life, death, and rebirth.

Prabhupada founded his movement known as ISKCON to spread his teachings throughout the world.

The last two decades of his life were spent in Puri immersing himself in devotional yearning for Kṛṣṇa and his consorts. Chairanya Jayanthi is celebrated on Phalgun poornima in many parts of our country especially in Bengal, Orissa, Bihar and Jharkhand. This festival is also known as Gaura Purnima.

HAMSA Krishnapriya: Major Sandeep Unnikrishnan was an officer in the Indian Army’s National Security Guard (NSG) and played a crucial role in the 2008 Mumbai attacks. Born on March 15, 1977, in Kerala, he was raised in Bangalore and later joined the National Defence Academy. Commissioned into the Bihar Regiment in 1999, he later became part of the NSG, specializing in counter-terrorism operations.

His life and fearless heroism were recently depicted in the 2022 film Major.

Key Events in Bhagat Singh’s Revolutionary Journey:

1. In 1926, he founded the Naujawan Bharat Sabha to mobilize youth against British rule.

2. In 1928, he joined protests against the Simon Commission, which led to police brutality and Lala Lajpat Rai’s death. Seeking revenge, he and his comrades mistakenly killed John Saunders instead of James Scott.

3. In 1929, he and Batukeshwar Dutt bombed the Central Legislative Assembly, protesting the Public Safety Bill and Trade Dispute Bill, chanting "Inquilab Zindabad!" before surrendering.

4. While in prison, he led a hunger strike for equal rights for Indian prisoners.

5. He also formed HSRA, where revolutionaries like Ram Prasad Bismil, Ashfaqullah Khan, and Chandrashekhar Azad were part.

Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev were sentenced to death in the Lahore Conspiracy Case. They were executed on March 23, 1931. Their bodies were secretly cremated, and their ashes thrown into the Sutlej River. March 23 is observed as Shaheed Diwas in their honor.

Apart from his paintings, he is also known for his rhythmic line drawings and illustrations. He studied at Nutan Kala Mandir, an art school and at Sir J. J. School of Art, Bombay and completed specialization in mural painting and worked as an art teacher in the same school. Hebbar was also well versed in music and dance. He had learned Kathak from Pandit Sunder Prasad. Hebbar's portrait of Abul Kalam Azad is housed in the Parliament House, New Delhi and other public collections are displayed in the National Gallery of Modern Art, New Delhi. Hebbar was selected a fellow of the Lalit Kala Akademi and served as the chairman of the Artist’s Centre, Bombay.He was honoured with Padma Bhushan.

"Art can be addressed to the artistic sensibility of the viewer. An artist, being a part of human society, wants his works to be communicative, though not in a sense of telling a story, teaching a moral or describing nature's grandeur. If a work of art displays technical perfection and also expresses a certain mood, thought or idea, communication becomes more meaningful." – K.K.Hebbar.

Krishnamohan Pasumarthi: Padma Shri Kalamandalam Krishnan Nair (27-03-1914 to 15-08-1990)

Born into a poor Nair family, Krishnan Nair was a renowned Kathakali dancer who was initiated into Kathakali under the tutelage of Guru Chandu Panikker. He had his higher studies on Rasa-abhinaya (facial emotions which stressed on eye exercises) from the Kudiyattam maestro Natyacharya Mani Madhava Chakyar. Kerala poet laureate Vallathol Narayana Menon got him enrolled as one of the first students of Kerala Kalamandalam which was a turning point for him. Krishnan Nair was known as Poothana Krishnan for his outstanding portrayals of Poothana (Poothanamoksham). His romantic roles like Nala Bheema, Arjuna, Rukmangada are enough proofs of his versatility. Kathakali lovers passionately hailed him as ‘Kalamandalam. He was honoured with Sangeet Natak Academy award and Padma Shri.

During a practice session, when Krishnan Nair fell and broke his thighbone and was bed-ridden his guru Panicker consoled him by saying, “You will be alright soon and in Kathakali you are going to be like butter in curdled milk". On this episode Krishnan Nair commented: "Those words were like “nectar” to me. Believe it or not, my recovery was fast and I resumed my training within a few months.”

త్యాగరాజ కీర్తనలలో కనిపించే  సంగీతజ్ఞానం. భక్తి, అనురక్తి, శరణాగతి, ఆర్తి, ఆవేదన, ఆనందం ఇలా అన్ని గుణాలు ఈ సినిమాలో ఆణువణువునా కనిపిస్తాయి. త్యాగయ్య కృత్తుల సర్వస్వానికి ఈ సినిమా ఒక దృశ్యరూపం అనడం అతిశయోక్తి  కాదేమో. 

రాముని కల్యాణగుణాలను కీర్తించడానికి కావలసిన సంగీత జ్ఞానం కోసం ఆయన పడిన తపన, ఆశువుగా గానం చేసే వేళ ఆనందం,

రాజాశ్రయాన్ని కాదన్నప్పుడు  నిబ్బరం, విగ్రహాలను పోగొట్టుకున్నప్పుడు ఆవేదన, అవి దొరకినపుడు ఆనందం, ఆశ్చర్యం, ముదిమిలో రాముని నగుమోమును గనలేని జాలి – ఇలా త్యాగయ్య జీవితం లోని అన్ని ఘట్టాలలో అద్భుతంగా పాడేరు, నటించేరు, జీవించేరు నాగయ్యగారు. 

సినిమాని సామవేదంతో ప్రారంభించి భజగోవిందంతో ముగించడం దర్శకునిగా అయన దర్శనానికి, ప్రతిభకి నిదర్శనం. సినిమా అంటే ఆషామాషి కాదని, సినిమా తియ్యడం ఒక యాగమని, యోగమని ఆయన నిరూపించారు. 

పన్నెండేళ్ల వయసులో మొదటి సారి త్యాగయ్య సినిమా చూసి చాలా సరదా పడ్డాను ఏమి తెలియకపోయినా. ఆ తర్వాత చాలా సార్లు చూసాను.

ఎన్నోసార్లు విన్నాను. ఇప్పటికి  ఆ సినిమాను తలుచుకొంటే ఒళ్ళు పులకరిస్తుంది. సామాన్యులకే కాక సంగీతవిద్వాంసులకు కూడా త్యాగయ్య మీద గౌరవాన్ని పెంచేలా తీశారు నాగయ్యగారు. ఈ మాటను సంగీత విద్వాంసులు నాతో స్వయంగా అన్నారు. త్యాగయ్యే నాగయ్య, నాగయ్యే త్యాగయ్య అని అనేవారట ఆరోజుల్లో. 

యోగి వేమన లో శృంగార, భక్తి, వైరాగ్యలను సమర్ధవంతంగా పోషించారు నాగయ్య గారు. అందాలు చిందేటి నాజ్యోతి, జీవహింస మానండి, కనిపించుమురా – ఇలా ఎన్నో  రసరమ్యగీతాలు పాడేరు. సాధారణంగా వీరి పాటలు విలంబంగా, నెమ్మదిగా ఉంటాయి. కానీ వదలజాలరా అనే పాటని చాలా వేగంగా, ఉత్సాహంగా పాడతారు. స్పీడు సంగతులు భలేగా ఉంటాయి. పద్యం పాడడంలో నాగయ్య గారి బాణి  విశిష్టమైనది. 

నాటకాలలో పద్యాలకు భిన్నంగా సినిమాకు తగినట్లు సంగీత సాహిత్య సమాతుకంతో పాడడం అయన ప్రారంభించారు. వేమన పద్యాలను గొప్పగా పాడారు. “తరుహీన” లాంటి పద్యాన్ని నాగయ్య గారు మాత్రమే పాడగలరు. 

భక్తపోతన మరొక అపురూప చిత్రం.

పోతన మహాకవి భక్తితత్వాన్ని పుణికి పుచ్చు కొని అద్భుతం గా నటించారు. ఈ సినిమా లోని “సర్వమంగళ నామ సీతా రామ” పాట ఆనాడు ఆంధ్రప్రదేశ్ లోని వాడ వాడలా భజనల్లో వినిపించేదట. “పావనగుణరామ, నన్ను విడచి కదలకుర, రామరామ సీతారామ” ఈ పాటలను ఇళ్లలో పెద్దవాళ్ళు పాడేవారు. భాగవతం పద్యాలు చదవడంలో ప్రత్యేకత చూపించే వారు. “ఎవ్వనించే జనించు” పద్యంలో - వాని ఈశ్వరు అని ఆగి – నే శరణంబు అని పాడేరు. అలాగే  “కాటుక కంటి నీరు” పద్యంలో కిరాతకులకు అమ్మా అని విడదీసి పాడతారు. పోతన గారు ఇలాగే ఉండేవారేమోననిపిస్తుంది నాగయ్యగారిని చూస్తే. అసలు సినిమా ప్రారంభంలో భగవద్గీత వింటేనే పరవశించిపోతాం.

Krishnamohan Pasumarthy: India's first war of Independence

Mangal Pandey was an Indian soldier who played an important role in incidents prior to the uprising of 1857, known as ‘India’s First War of Independence.

Pandey served the British East India Company as a sepoy in its 34th Bengal Native Infantry (BNI) regiment. He refused to bite off ends of greased cartridges of a newly introduced Enfield rifle after the rumours of use of cow and pig fat as a lubricant in the cartridges. The infuriated man thereafter incited his comrades to rebel against the British officers.

On the afternoon of 29 March 1857, Lieutenant Baugh, Adjutant of the 34th Bengal Native Infantry stationed at Barrackpore was informed that several men of his regiment including Mangal Pandey who was calling upon the men to rebel and threatening to shoot the first European that he set eyes on. Baugh armed himself and galloped on his horse to the lines while Pandey took position and fired at at Baugh but missed. Pandey attacked him with a talwar and slashed Baugh and brought him to the ground. Then British Seargent Major Hewson had charged towards Pandey but he was knocked to the ground from behind by a blow from Pandey's musket. Pandey attrempted to shoot himself after having been restrained and eventually was overpowered and arrested. He was executed on 8th April, 1857.

Pandey has been remembered as a freedom fighter against British rule. A commemorative postage stamp with his image on it was issued by the Indian Post.

Krishnamohan Pasumarthy: Dr. Anandi Gopal Joshi (31-03-1865 to 26-02-1887)

Dr. Anandi was India's first woman doctor. Her son's death at the age of fourteen days due to lack of medical care ignited a burning desire in her to study medicine and prevent such tragedies from befalling other women. She realized the dire need for female doctors in India, particularly for women. Theodicia Carpenter, an American, agreed to sponsor Anandi’s education in America where she graduated with an MD degree, making her the first Indian woman to achieve this feat. Her thesis highlighted the practices and challenges faced by Indian women during childbirth and emphasized the need for trained female medical professionals in India. After returning to India where she was appointed at the Albert Edward Hospital in Kolhapur. She played active role in promoting women’s health and education and their improved healthcare facilities. Her legacy continues to motivate efforts towards gender equality and the empowerment of women. A commemorative stamp in her honor was issued by the Government.
